Kabuli Palaw


Ingredients

  • 2 cups basmati rice
  • 500g lamb or beef
  • 2 carrots(julienned)
  • 1/2 cup raisins
  • 1 medium onion
  • 1/4 cup oil
  • 1 tsp cumin powder
  • 1 tsp cinnamon
  • salt and pepper to taste

Cooking Instructions

  1. Rinse and soak the basmati rice in water for 30 minutes.
  2. In a pot, heat oil and fry the chopped onion until golden.
  3. Add meat and brown it.Add salt,pepper, and 1 cup of water. cover and simmer until tender.
  4. In another pan, fry carrots and raisins lightly in oil and set aside.
  5. Boil and soaked rice in salted water until half cooked. Drain.
  6. Layer the meat and ricein a pot.Top with fried carrots and raisins. Sprinkle cumin and cinnamon.
  7. Cover tightly and steam on low heat for 20-30 minutes.
  8. Serve warm with salad or chutney.

Ferni


Ingredients

  • 2 cups milk
  • 3 tablespoones cornstrach
  • 4 tablespoones sugar(adjust to taste)
  • 1/2 teaspoon cardamom powder
  • 1 tablespoon rose water(optional)
  • Crushed pistachios or almonds for garnish.

Cooking Instructions

  1. In a small bowl, mix cornstrach with 1/4 cup of cold milk to make a smooth slurry.
  2. In a saucepan, heat the remaining milk over medium heat.
  3. Add sugar and stir until fully dissolved.
  4. Slowly add the cornstrach slurry to the hot milk while continuously stirring to avoid lumps.
  5. Cook and stir until the mixture thickens to a pudding-like consistency (about 5-7 minutes).
  6. Add cardamom powder and rose water. stir well.
  7. Pour the mixture into small serving bowls and left it cool to room temperature.
  8. Refrigerate for at least 2 hours to set completely.
  9. Garnish with crushed nuts before serving.

Bolani


Ingredients

  • 2 cups all-purpose flour
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt
  • 3/4 cup water
  • 1 cup mashed boiled potatoes
  • 1/2 cup chopped green onions
  • 1/2 chopped fresh cilantro
  • 1 green chili
  • 1/2 teaspoon black pepper
  • salt to taste
  • oil for frying

Cooking Instruction

  1. In a bowl, mix flour and salt.Gradually add water and knead into soft dough. cover and let rest for 30 minutes.
  2. In another bowl, mix mashed potatoes, green onions, cilantro, chili, salt,and pepper to make the filling.
  3. Divide the dough into small balls. roll each ball into a thin circle on a floured surface.
  4. Place 2-3 tablespoon of filling on one half of the circle. fold over and seal the edges by pressing gently.
  5. Heat oil on a skillet over medium heat.Fry each bolani until goldenbrown and crispy on both sides.
  6. place on paper towels to remove excess oil.
  7. serve warm with yogurt or chutney.
